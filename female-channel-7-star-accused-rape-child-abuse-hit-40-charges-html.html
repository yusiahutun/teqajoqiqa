Female Channel 7 star accused of rape and child abuse is hit with 40 more charges

February 27, 2024
blog

A Channel 7 television personality accused of a string of offences including rape has been slapped with an additional 40 charges. 

Proceedings against the woman, who cannot be publicly named for legal reasons, continued in Richlands Magistrates Court, in Queensland, on Tuesday. 

The court heard the woman has been hit with an additional 40 charges on top of an existing raft of allegations, including rape and serious child abuse offences, the Courier Mail reported.  

According to court documents, the woman was charged with 38 of the new charges on November 7 this year and another on November 10, meaning she now faces some 80 charges. 

A woman who appeared on a Channel 7 show has been hit with 40 fresh charges, meaning has is now facing some 80 charges 

One charge of common assault has since been dropped. 

The charges date back several years and span several locations across Queensland.  

They include include further rape and child abuse charges as well as allegations of observations or recordings in breach of privacy genital or anal region, torture, disabling in order to commit and indictable offence and assault occasioning bodily harm.

The TV personality's defence lawyer requested an adjournment in order to gather more material on the fresh charges. 

The woman's most recent court appearance follows a previous ruling that she could not be named by the media out of fears she might self harm after coverage of the high-profile case by Channel 9's A Current Affair.

It also came after she attempted to self harm last month.

The woman's identity has been withheld out of fears she might self harm (stock image pictured) 

The case has been one of the first to put Queensland's new sexual offence identification laws to the test. 

As of October 3, persons accused of, and charged with sexual offences, including rape, attempted rape, assault with intent to commit rape, and sexual assault, can have their identity published by the media.

The woman's partner was also in October charged with two counts each of torture and common assault and one charge each of observations or recordings in breach of privacy and assault occasioning bodily harm.

The partner also cannot be publicly named.  

Magistrate Aaron Simpson increased the woman's bail and the matter has been set down for committal mention on January 23, 2024.
