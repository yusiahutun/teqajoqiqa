Cricketer Michael Bevans daughter Liv auditions for The Voice

October 04, 2024
blog

While the daughter of cricket legend Michael Bevan and director of the McGrath Foundation Tracy Bevan lives in the spotlight, her high-profile connections won't help win over judges during her blind audition for The Voice.

Liv Bevan, 19, chose to apply for the Nine reality series in order to chase her dream of becoming a singer and to carve her own success outside of the family.

In an interview with NineHoney on Wednesday, Liv said: 'I love performing because it’s just mine; it’s my thing and it makes me feel really good about myself.'

Michael, a former Australian cricketer, and ex-wife Tracy signed Liv up for singing lessons at the age of seven during their 21-years of marriage. 

Since then, her passion for the stage has only grown, igniting a dream of pursuing a singing career.

While it has always been a hobby for the young hopeful, she told the publication it was only recently that she wondered whether she could make a living from it.

'I went to University and studied PR and realised one day, on a bus ride into the city, that I didn't want to be like a lot of people around me who were going to jobs that they didn't enjoy,' Liv recalled.

'I didn't want to be like a lot of people... going to jobs that they didn't enjoy': While singing has always been a hobby for the young hopeful, Liv said it was only recently that she wondered whether she could make a living from it

Taking a break from study, she returned from a travelling stint with a renewed focus - to chase her dream and sign up for The Voice.

She shared a promo clip of the show's season return to Instagram, but remained vague on how she performs.

Liv told the publication she was hopeful of landing a spot on Joe Jonas' team. 

'Joe probably knows a lot about how to break away from your family's success and be successful on your own… because he has been,' she said. 

However, the singer admitted being around highly successful people had always spurred her on: 'Having people close to me who are successful inspires me and gives me the strength to aim high, too.'

Liv's blind audition will air on Monday, but it's unsure whether a judge chair will turn around to offer her a place in the Knockout rounds.

Three rounds of blind auditions have already aired, with 15 contestants being selected to progress to Knockouts. 

The Voice airs on Channel Nine on Sunday at 7pm and Monday and Tuesday at 7.30pm
