How to write a letter to your friend inviting him to attend your birthday party

Going traditional might be the way if you have an upcomingbirthday party and want to make it exceptional. And where do you start? The invites. Invite a friend over to your birthday party via a letter, which you can send through the post. However, there are different formats and things to keep in mind.

1. Determine the nature of the occasion

Since the letter is an invitation to your birthday, you don’t have to think this part over. It is always a great idea to keep this in mind before drafting your letter to keep everything in context and not stray away.

2. Include all required details

Brainstorm before writing your letter to ascertain that you have all the required details. These details include the event, time, place and attire if needed. You can also mention that meals and snacks will be served on your birthday, or people should carry their bottles of wine. Another thing you should remember is the map of your location.

3. State if a response is required

Does the invitation letter require a response? If so, include an R.S.V.P. so that people can confirm their attendance for the event. Including this element in the birthday invitation letter will make it easier for you to know the number of expected guests.

Steps on how to write a birthday invitation letter

An invitation for friends to a birthday party shouldn’t be crafted as you do an official letter but should be formal. Also, it is similar to how you compose standard letters but with slight differences here and there.

1. Create the subject line

The subject line is for those who are composing an email. The subject section, of course, shows the essence of your letter. So, since it is a birthday party invitation message for a friend, it can be: “Invitation to a birthday party.”

2. Add your letterhead

Letterheads give your letter an identity. However, leave it blank if you have nothing to add here since it is not an organisational event.

3. Add the sender’s address

After the upper section is complete, mention the sender’s address. This address is where the guests are going to send their R.S.V.P.s.

4. Write the date and recipient’s address

After you have included the sender’s address, write the date of sending the birthday invitations. Here, you can mention the date however you like. After the date, write the recipient’s address along with their name. Including their name and address assures them they are the ones being invited.

5. Include the salutation

Start the letter with a salutation; you can customise it for all your friends. You can include an inside joke to make it hilarious. End the greeting with a coma and skip a line.

6. Compose the main body

The main body is the primary part of your letter and should start with phrases like “I am pleased to welcome you….” “It would be a pleasure for us if you could come….”

7. Write the closing and signature and proofread the letter

Close the birthday invitation letter with closing remarks and express gratitude towards the invitation recipient. Remember to proofread your letter to eliminate mistakes.
