Courses offered at Adventist University of Africa

June 09, 2024
blog

Adventist University of Africa (AUA) is an educational institution that offers practical programmes in the fields of theology, church leadership, and pastoral ministry. The learning centre has postgraduate courses that help to impart knowledge, skills, and attitude to those that want to take an active leadership role in the church and other areas. AUA strives to deliver postgraduate education in a Christian context, even in the areas of business, health, and technology.

By offering religious courses, AUA helps to prepare learners to render service-based leadership in their respective areas of work. Although the university offers many postgraduate programmes, it is one of the largest Christian institutions in Africa for studying Theology. As a student, you can join the Adventist University of Africa to study religious and other leadership courses.

Main courses offered at Adventist University of Africa

These are practical programmes offered at AUA and their admission requirements:

1. Master of Arts in Biblical and Theological Studies

The university designed the programme to help pastors, theologians, and other individuals to acquire expertise in the areas of church history, theology, and scripture. Its qualification requirements are:

  • A bachelor’s degree in Theology.
  • At least 72 credit hours of the theology course.
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0.
  • Proof of proficiency in Greek Grammar or biblical Hebrew.

Applicants who do not show proficiency in Greek Grammar or Biblical Hebrew will have to take a bridging course to qualify.

2. Master of Arts in Leadership

The Master of Arts in Leadership at AUA is a course designed for individuals that want to advance their leadership skills in areas of business, church administration, health care, education, technology, and more. It covers many subjects, including financial leadership, coaching, organisation, and management, among others. Its admission requirements include:

  • A bachelor's degree from a recognised university.
  • A minimum GPA (Grade Point Average) of 3.0 or equivalent.
  • Four years of work experience at a professional level.
  • A recommendation letter from the university from which you earned your bachelor’s degree.
  • Ability to study in English.

3. Master of Arts in Missiology

It is a programme for pastors and other individuals that want to be experts in the field of Missiology. The course covers both Islamic Studies and African Traditional Religions. To qualify, a student must:

  • Meet the general admission requirements for a Master’s Programme.
  • Have a degree in Theology or Religion.
  • Have at least 28 credits of General Education courses and 2.5 GPA.
  • Have four years of work experience from a relevant field.

A student must also have a recommendation letter from a former university or employing organisation to qualify for the programme.

4. Master of Arts in Pastoral Theology

The programme is for pastors that want to advance their education as ministers to improve ministerial functions at the church. It is also suitable for those that want to increase their knowledge of theological and biblical studies. Its admission requirements are:

  • A bachelor’s degree in Religion or Theology from a recognised university.
  • At least 28 credits of General Education course and a minimum GPA of 2.5.
  • At least four years of professional work experience.
  • A recommendation letter from a previous employing organisation or university.

Students that enrol for the programme will benefit from an array of on-campus and e-library resources.

5. Master of Business Administration (MBA)

The MBA programme at AUA is for individuals that want to get a higher degree to improve their critical thinking and professional development. Students can choose from various study areas, including finance, accounting, management, and healthcare management, among others. Its qualification requirements are:

  • A bachelor’s degree from a recognised university.
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 or its equivalent.
  • Two years of postgraduate work experience.

A letter of recommendation from employing organisation or former university must accompany the application.

6. Master of Chaplaincy

The programme is for individuals that want to gain knowledge and prepare themselves for chaplaincy ministries or for pastors and chaplains that want to study a higher degree. To qualify for the course, one needs the following requirements:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Religion or Theology.
  • Minimum GPA of 2.5 or 3.0 for direct students.
  • Recommendation letter from employing organisation or former university.

A student must also meet the general requirements for admission into a Master’s programme.

7. Master of Divinity

The programme is for students that want to gain both practical and theoretical knowledge of the Bible in its entirety. Its admission requirements include:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Religion or Theology.
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0.
  • Statement of Purpose for pursuing the programme.
  • Transcripts to show completion of Biblical Language courses.
  • Passing a proficiency exam of various relevant courses provided by the Adventist in University Rongai.

Students must also submit recommendation letters from a Church board where they are members.

8. Master of Public Health (MPH)

The programme is for individuals that want to advance their studies in the area of public health. Students can select from three areas of study that include Environmental Health, Health Promotion, and Nutrition. Its qualification requirements include:

  • A bachelor’s degree from a relevant field of study.
  • A minimum GPA of 3.0.
  • Work experience of at least four years or proof of research capability for students with a GPA of 2.50.
  • Demonstration of the ability to study in English.

An applicant must also meet specific prerequisites taken while doing their undergraduate degree. These include 3-semester credits in biology or human anatomy and physiology and human nutrition.

9. Master of Science in Applied Computer Science

The course is for anybody that wants to develop their knowledge in the area of information technology and security. The areas of specialisation for the programme are Cyber Security, E-services, and Networks and Systems Administration. To enrol, a student must meet the following requirements.

10. Doctor of Philosophy, Religion

A PhD in religion from AUA helps to develop educated scholars who can contribute their knowledge and apply the correct biblical methodologies to educational institutions. The qualification criteria are:

  • A Masters of Arts in Biblical and Theological Studies, Divinity, or Missiology.
  • A minimum of 42-semester credits.
  • A minimum Cumulative Grade Point Average of 3.33.
  • Completion of at least six credits of coursework in research at a postgraduate level.
  • Submission of a completed and accepted project or thesis.
  • Evidence of knowledge of biblical Hebrew and Greek Grammar
  • At least four years of experience in a working field or church ministry.
  • Three recommendation letters. These include professional, academic, and student’s character recommendation.

11. Doctor of Philosophy, Leadership

The PhD in Leadership programme is for individuals that want to improve their knowledge, skills, and power as competent leaders. To qualify, one must have the following:

  • A Master’s degree in Leadership or relevant.
  • A minimum of 42-semester credits.
  • A minimum CGPA of 3.33.
  • Completion of at least six credits of coursework in statistics and research at a postgraduate level.
  • Submission of a completed and accepted project or thesis.
  • Three recommendation letters. These include professional, academic, and character recommendation.

An applicant must also submit a statement of purpose when completing their application.

Adventist University of Africa fees structure

The fee structure at AUA varies depending on the programme you want to pursue. A student will have to pay around $745 for one year (12 credits) to complete their Master’s course. For PhD students, it will cost them $3,870 for one year (12 credits).

The institution also has other charges, including exam, registration, and ICT fees, among others. To get more details about the entire fee structure, read AUA’s financial information from its website.

Adventist University of Africa scholarships

AUA is a Seventh Day Adventist University that recognises the financial hardships of many African students who have a strong commitment to learning. To help them out, the institution has a scholarship fund for individuals that need financial support to achieve their educational needs. A person can qualify for the fund if they meet the guidelines set by AUA.
