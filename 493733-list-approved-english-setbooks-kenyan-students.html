Approved English setbooks in Kenyan schools

Compulsory English setbooks in 2024

Compulsory English set books are ones that every student must read and are examined by Kenya National Examinations Council. KCID approved two mandatory set books for 2022 until 2026.

1. Fathers of Nations by Paul B. Vitta

Fathers of Nations (published by Oxford University) by Paull B. Vitta is the new compulsory novel in Kenyan learning institutions. The book replaces Blossoms of the Savanna by Henry Ole Kulet.

The biggest highlight of the piece of literature is a summit attended at Gambia by African Heads of State. It is important to note that Fathers of Nations is a satirical novel set in contemporary Africa.

The plot rotates around four men from different parts of Africa. It analyses their various problems and how they got together and attempted to make African heads of state correct a document that could transform Africa's economic fortunes.

2. The Samaritan by John Lara

The new play from the rest of the world introduced into Kenyan schools is The Samaritan, written by John Lara and published by the Kenya Literature Bureau. The piece of literature replaces A Doll’s House by Henry Ibsen.

The Samaritan is set in Marcas Municipality, a fictional state that tells the story of two students who developed a mobile application called The Samaritan. It is an online platform for whistleblowing and sharing ideas and information for enhancing public welfare.

Optional English set books in Kenya

The good thing about English Literature subject in Kenya is that students have optional set books. So, what are the optional books in Kenyan schools?

1. Artist of the Floating World by Kazuo Ishiguro

Artist of the Floating World by Kazuo Ishiguro and published by Longhorn Publishers is the new optional novel replacing The Pearl by John Steinbeck. The exciting piece of literature is set in post-World War II Japan and is narrated by Masuji Ono.

The narrator looks back on his life and how he has lived it and provides a fascinating account. The book is regarded as historical fiction and global literature. It is considered historical fiction because it is based on the past that predates the writer. On the other hand, it is regarded as global literature because of its vast international market.

2. A Silent Song and Other Stories

A Silent Song and Other Stories is an exciting publication edited by Godwin Siundu and published by Spotlight Publishers (EA) Limited. The optional anthology replaces Memories We Lost by Chris Wanjala.

The book has short stories composed by different authors. Every story has a distinct theme, plot, setting, and the language and narrations are also different. One of the most exciting things about the book’s stories is that they are short, which means there are fewer characters, short plots, and fewer stylistic devices.

3. A Parliament of Owls by Adipo Sidang

A Parliament of Owls by Adipo Sidang is the new optional play replacing Inheritance by David Mulwa. It is one exciting read as it possesses poetry works of the author touching on different themes and the status of African politics as the over-arching theme.

How to verify if your set book is original

The new set books are available for purchase from designated bookstores in Kenya, but you must ascertain that you only procure an original copy from the publishers. Therefore, the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development has further protected consumers from buying fakes by providing certain security features.

First, all original books possess a footnote stating, “Approved by KICD 2022 Edition”. On top of this, all books have a section where you can scratch to reveal a 16-digit PIN. You can send the 16-digit PIN to 22776 to verify originality. You will get SMS feedback notifying you whether you have an original copy.
