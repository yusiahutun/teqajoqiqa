Some people choose to buy homes, while others prefer to build on their own. While the latter is a majority's dream, it is one of the most head-scratching projects one could ever undertake. But luckily, it is all worth it in the end. So, what is the cost of building a 4-bedroom maisonette in Kenya?

The cost of building a 4-bedroom maisonette in Kenya varies according to various factors. The location of your construction site, desired design, preferred material and size are some things to consider before embarking on this journey.

What is a 4-bedroom maisonette?

A four-bedroom maisonette typically comprised of the following key features:

  • Spacious living room
  • Adjoining dining room
  • 4 bedrooms and 3 bathrooms
  • Master en suite bedroom upstairs
  • 2 additional bedrooms upstairs sharing a bathroom
  • 1 Bedroom downstairs
  • 1 Shared bathroom downstairs

Cost of building a 4-bedroom maisonette in Kenya

There is no standard amount set because the construction cost will vary depending on several factors. Once you know these variables, it will be easier to get a rough estimate. The average cost of building a house depends on several factors. Some of the variables include:

1. Location

The location of your construction is one of the critical variables in determining how much the house will cost. If the location is far from the material sources, you will use more money to transport materials to the construction site.

In addition, if the site has red soil, it will cost you less because it doesn't need to be worked on or moved, unlike black cotton soil. Also, building your house in wetlands will cost you more since you need to build a stronger foundation.

2. Rooms size

The size of the rooms you decide to build affects the overall cost of building your maisonette. Smaller rooms equate to a smaller house area and overall reduced cost. On the other hand, larger rooms equal increased house area and, in turn, increased cost.

For instance, a low-cost maisonette has an estimated cost of around Ksh 25,000 per square metre. Therefore, a 100-metre-square house will cost you Ksh 2.5 million. If you increase the square metres to 150, you will spend around Ksh 3.75 million. Lastly, if you go for a standard house, the cost per square metre is Ksh 35,000; therefore, you will spend Ksh 5.25 million to build a 150-square-metre house.

3. Preferred design and height of the house

The design you choose for your house significantly affects the overall money you spend on construction. Some of the house designs that people choose include:

  • Balconies
  • Studio on the upper floor
  • Front or back porch
  • Garage

Concerning the height of your structure, even though higher ceilings and floors make the house elegant and more spacious, they come at an extra cost. The additional height will require stronger structural elements and more materials for the walls and finishes. In turn, this will increase the construction cost.

4. Materials

The materials you choose, from founding the house to painting, will affect the amount of money you spend on construction. For instance, the cost of a brick wall will be different from stones and blocks. In addition, the electrical and plumbing materials will vary depending on the type.

5. Cost of handbook

The construction handbook will also determine the cost of building a 4-bedroom maisonette as it varies from one area to another. The handbook is a development of the Institute of Quantity Surveyors of Kenya calculated per square metre of construction.

However, before building, you will also need the services of a quantity surveyor to give you an estimation of the budget in the:

  • Pre-contract stage
  • Cost control
  • Planning during construction

Is it cheaper to build a maisonette or a bungalow?

It is cheaper to build a bungalow than a maisonette of the same size and finishes. The biggest difference in overall cost is because a bungalow only requires a foundation, while a maisonette will require both a foundation and slab.
