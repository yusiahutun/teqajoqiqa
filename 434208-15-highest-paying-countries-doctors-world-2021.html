15 highest-paying countries for doctors in the world in 2021

May 28, 2024

To qualify to be a doctor, it takes numerous years of expensive study and practice. No wonder most countries around the world pay doctors exceptionally well to compensate them for their struggle. Working as a physician in the highest-paying countries for doctors is well worth the tension, hard work, and schooling.

Which country pays doctors the most? It is hard to define what typical doctors salaries are. There are a number of factors to take into consideration. First, it depends on the country in which you are located. Pay scales for doctors are also different depending on the area of speciality that doctor practices in. The specialists earn more than the general practitioners.

List of the highest-paying countries for doctors in the world

Doctors, especially surgeons, attract big paycheques compared to most other professions because of the challenging nature of their work. The salary for doctors varies across countries depending on the speciality. Here is a breakdown of the average doctor salary by country.

1. United States - $294,000 per year

The US is one of the countrieswhere doctors make the most money. To qualify and work as a doctor in the US, you must complete and pass the United States Medical Licensing Exam (USMLE).

Overall, across all states and medical specialities, the annual mean salary of a doctor in the United States is $294,000 a year. However, the doctor salaries vary based on location and speciality.

2. Canada - $278,000 per year

Canada is consistently ranked as one of the best countries in the world to live in. However, it faces a huge shortage of doctors because most of them relocate to the US in search of better working conditions and higher pay.

As a result, the few remaining doctors are forced to work for longer hours and to attend to a huge number of patients.

In Canada, the salary of a doctor depends on which province you live in, what your specialization is, and how many years you spend in school. Doctor salary in Canada ranges from $278,000 per year to more than $769,000.

3. Switzerland - $275,124 per year

Switzerland is one of the most developed countries in the world, boasting the highest nominal wealth per adult. It has one of the best healthcare systems globally, which boasts a network of highly-skilled doctors, efficient staff, and quality facilities.

A person working as a doctor or physician in Switzerland typically earns around $275,124 per year. However, certain specialists, such as neurosurgeons, take home a median salary of $746,030 per year.

4. Luxembourg - $253,000 per year

This is a small country located in Western Europe. It hosts some of the highest-paid doctors in the world. Living and working in Luxembourg is a dream come true for any doctor. In order to work as a specialist or general practitioner in Luxembourg, the doctor must have a conventional doctor's diploma.

5. The Netherlands - $253,000 per year

The Netherlands has one of the best healthcare systems in the world. You will be surprised to know that each household in the country has its own doctor.

Doctors working in The Netherlands enjoy great working conditions, including shorter work hours and plenty of holidays. Specialists pocket around $253,000 annually, while general practitioners make an average of $117,000 annually.

6. Norway - $196,834 per year

Norway is one of the most advanced countries in the world in terms of modern healthcare infrastructure. The advanced healthcare system implies good working conditions for doctors and other healthcare workers. On average, a doctor in Norway works around eight hours a day.

As of 2021, the average annual pay for a doctor or physician in Norway is $196,834 per year. There are many opportunities for advancement and increased pay based on skill level, location and years of experience.

7. Denmark - $195,741

Due to high living standards, a robust welfare system and a healthy balance between work and private life, Denmark is one of the best countries to work and live in. A doctor in Denmark works for an average of 37 hours per week. The salary for doctors in Denmark is estimated at $195,741 per year.

8. Finland - $175,674 per year

With its progressive politics, great healthcare system, and overall quality of living, Finland is one of the most appealing destinations for doctors. Usually, doctors work in 8 hours shifts day time and on-call from 8 to 24 hours. The average pay for a physician or doctor is $175,674 a year or $84.29 per hour in Finland.

9. Belgium - $171,183

Belgium is of the countries that compensate its doctors well. However, the doctors work the longest hour compared to other countries in Europe. On average, the doctor works for 51 hours per week.

10. Germany - $166,000

In comparison to other countries, Germany has a highly effective healthcare system. Using the latest treatment methods and modern medical instruments allows for a high level of care. The doctors in the country are paid well. The average pay for a medical doctor in Germany is $166,000 per year.

11. Austria - $163,677 per year

Austria has one of the best reputations in the world for its healthcare system, and doctors in Austria are the cream of the crop. A doctor working in Austria earns an average salary of $163,677 per year.

To qualify and work as a doctor in Austria, you must be registered with ÖÄK (Österreichische Ärztekammer), which is a competent authority for all doctors in the country.

12. Australia - $156,000 per year

This is a perennial favourite of doctors planning to work overseas, and it's easy to see why. The quality health system makes Australia a good place for doctors to work and live. Doctors in the country work fewer hours than in the UK—usually between 35 and 38 hours a week, depending on seniority and speciality.

The average salary for doctors in Australia is $156,000 per year. Entry-level positions start at $129,188 per year, while the most experienced doctors make up to $234,000 per year.

13. France - $130,361 per year

France is a perfect haven for doctors as it pays them well. Doctors work on average between 38 and 39 hours a week, but they are compensated for working overtime.

The average salary for a doctor working in France is around $130,361 per year. It varies depending on seniority and the number of weekly working hours and includes the possibility of earning bonuses and allowances.

14. New Zealand - $105,828 per year

New Zealand has a modern, well-run, fair healthcare system, and doctors are well looked after. The country offers an unparalleled work-life balance for doctors as they work between 32 and 40 hours per week.

Doctors salary in New Zealand ranges between $49,350 and $415,271 per year. On average, a doctor receives an annual average salary of $105,828.

15. Ireland - $101,591 per year

Ireland scores consistently highly on best-paying countries for specialists and general practitioners. It has a reputation of giving mouth-watering offers to its doctors and is one of the countries of choice for doctors seeking a greener pasture.

A doctor working in Ireland earns an average salary of between $78,650- $101,122 annually. On average, doctors earn $101,591 per year.
