Santa Monica pizzeria DagWoods sells rainbow glitter unicorn pizza

June 04, 2024
blog

Just when we thought every possible unicorn food had been exhausted, a California restaurant has introduced glittery rainbow unicorn pizza.

DagWoods, an Italian restaurant on Wilshire Blvd. in Santa Monica, began serving up rainbow slices back in December, but has earned even more attention this month as diners flock to the eatery during Pride month.

The 'Magical AF' pizza is just a regular cheese-and-tomato-sauce pie, except the mozzarella on top is saturated in edible glitter sprinkled on in a rainbow design

Sparkle slice: DogWoods, an Italian restaurant in Santa Monica, California, is selling this 'Magical AF' pizza

Gooey glitter: It's just a regular cheese pizza with edible glitter sprinkled in a rainbow design on the cheese

Mystic pizza: They recently also had a mural of angel wings made out of slices of pizza added to the restaurant; naturally, it's become a quick Instagram hit

'Introducing the mythical, legendary, and #MagicalAF Pizza. Tasting is believing,' the restaurant wrote on Instagram in December when it debuted the item, uploading a shot of the pizza with an image of a unicorn vomiting rainbow colors.

The pie has pink, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple glitter starting at the crust and circling in to the middle.

A 9" small pie costs $12, a 12" medium is $19, an 16" large is $25, and a 24" X-Large is $41. 

The item first debuted for only a limited time, but came back by popular demand in April, after Jimmy Kimmel spotlighted the dish on his show. 

Holly Madison also got her hands on a pie back in December and shared a picture on Instagram.

DagWoods clearly knows a thing or two about appealing to Instagram-conscious diners. In addition to the sparkly pizza, the eatery also had a mural on one wall of a pair of angel's wings — rendered to look like they are made of pizza.

Visitors like to take pictures in front of the wings, painted by artist @dazhi_art, while holding their slices, as a pair of women wearing unicorn onesies did recently. 

While some social media commenters are skeptical that a pizza covered in glitter could taste good, those who've tried it and posted pictures online promise it's just like a regular cheese pizza.

While the pizza wasn't made specifically for Pride, quite a few restaurants, bakeries, and bars have been selling special rainbow food and drink this month. Though many of these aren't glittery-covered — or unicorn themed — they're certainly cheerful enough to be all over social media.
