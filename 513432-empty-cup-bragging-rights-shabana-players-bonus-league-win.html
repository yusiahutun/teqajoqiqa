As the season for National Super League (NSL) came to an end last weekend, a few days after the finale of the FKF Premier League, where Gor Mahia emerged victorious, the question left on many footballers’ and fans’ minds was - what about the winning bonus?

While Gor Mahia have been lucky enough to get support from their sponsor and well-wishers in terms of monetary tokens for winning the league, it remains unclear if Shabana will get the same.

The NSL Cup may be all Shabana players got for their weekend win in the NSL tournament, given that the club did not have a known sponsor.

However, in other leagues, such as Ghana Premier League, which ended on June 11, The story has been one-of-a-kind. Players are paid even for winning matches during the season.

How other league winners abroad were honoured

The league sponsor betPawa - which is owned by Kigali-based Mchezo Africa Limited, paid players of winning teams per match by remitting the money directly through their phones via MTN mobile money.

TUKo.co.ke understands that by the end of the league, the money so far successfully remitted to the players was KSh 7.3 million shillings.

The amount could have been higher, but some players have no MTN Mobile money accounts and this was being pursued at the time of publishing this piece.

Players from Medeama SC earned the highest as the team emerged the Ghana Premier League winners for season 2023 for the first time in the club’s history.

“It has really helped us a lot and it always motivates us and propels us to fight hard to win matches. On most occasions, my colleagues and I rely on the bonus to remit our families back home anytime there is the need to,” said Abdul Jelilu, Captain of Dreams FC.

In neighbouring Tanzania, the team that won the premier league - Yanga SC - bagged over KSh 220 million, which included payouts from NBC Bank who were the league’s main sponsor, Azam media which had contracts with Tanzania Football Federation as the league’s broadcaster and also in a separate contract directly with the club and also KSh 8.6 million from their shirt sponsor.
