Every K-pop concert coming up in the UK as Itzy, TXT and The Boyz festival axed

blog

The so-called "Korean Wave" – or Hallyu – is in full effect and the global popularity of K-Pop is higher than ever before.

Now, more and more music acts from South Korea are heading overseas and stopping off in the UK on their world tours.

Groups including BTS, Blackpink, The Boyz, ATEEZ, Dreamcatcher and soloists like Hyolyn and G-Dragon have already brought their charisma and powerful performances to the UK.

Sadly, two-day event KPOP Lux has been postponed, meaning fans will have to wait more for Itzy and TXT to make their UK debuts.

September 22-23, 2023 – KPOP LUX London – POSTPONED

KPOP Lux has sadly confirmed the two-day festival has been postponed, just days before it was scheduled to take place.

Headliners including Itzy, TXT and The Boyz were due to take to the stage in London, however it will now not go ahead as planned.

David Ciclitira, chairman of Live Company Group PLC, said: "Unfortunately, with another event occurring the same weekend in Europe, with the cost of living crisis ongoing, and the KPOP LUX Festival in-between two other KPOP events, ticket sales have not been sufficient enough to allow the event to go ahead. Furthermore, in order to ensure the show is made to the highest possible quality, we had shortened the festival to two days and prepared everything accordingly, but unfortunately it couldn't happen.

"We are truly sorry to all the fans, and we hope to be able to put on a show next year with more time to organise and for everyone to plan in advance."

September 28 – Aespa

Ever exciting girl group Aespa are heading to London as part of their first ever world tour – Synk: Hyper Line.

The quartet – Karina, Winter, Giselle and Ningning – will perform at London's O2 with tracks from My World, including recent release Spicy.

They will also storm the stage with fan favourites such as debut Black Mamba, Illusion and Next Level.

As part of the tour, Aespa will also perform around the world in Mexico, Brazil, Chile, Germany, France and the US.

November 8 - Korea On Stage In London

London is set to host another bumper K-Pop concert in November, with MyMusicTaste, who has brought countless groups to the UK, one of the promoters.

The first line-up announcement included P1Harmony, Stayc, Dreamcatcher, Jannabi and Yageum Yageum.

The event is being held to celebrate 140 years of UK-Korea relations.
