Prince Andrew's friend 'invited to Eugenie's wedding despite being investigated over rape claim'

March 31, 2024
blog

A billionaire friend of Prince Andrew was invited to Princess Eugenie's wedding even though he had been investigated over an alleged rape, reports claim.

Chinese internet tycoon Richard Liu, who sponsors one of the Duke of York's business initiatives, attended the October 12 ceremony with his wife Zhang Zetian.

The 45-year-old's appearance at Windsor Castle came six weeks after he was arrested in America on suspicion of 'criminal sexual conduct'.

It was the first time he had been seen in public with his 24-year-old wife of three years since the scandal broke.

Liu, who is worth an estimated £5.7billion, denies the allegation and was released without charge.

But questions have been raised about why he was invited to the wedding of Princess Eugenie and Jack Brooksbank.

US prosecutors are considering whether to pursue formal charges and he could also face a civil action from his alleged victim, The Times report.

The Duke of York has visited China at least seven times in the past 14 years.

Liu pictured after his arrest in America

In May, during a 10-day trip, he met Liu and visited the Beijing headquarters of his internet retail company JD.com.

He also met with President Xi Jinping.

Liu's wife has been the public face of the firm's fashion portfolio.

She shot to fame in China when a photo of her holding a glass of Taiwanese bubble tea went viral.

Another controversial guest at Princess Eugenie's wedding was convicted Libyan gun smuggler Tarek Kaituni.

The cost to taxpayers for security measures at the wedding was approximately £2million.

A Kensington Palace spokesperson told MailOnline: 'JD.com is a corporate partner of Pitch@Palace, an initiative set up by The Duke of York to champion entrepreneurship in the UK and overseas. 

'JD.com has been a long term strategic partner in the same way as a number of other organisations, providing resources, support and advocacy for the initiative and those it works with.' 

RICHARD LIU: INTERNET TYCOON WITH ESTIMATED £5.7BN FORTUNE

Born March 10, 1973 in Suqian, a poor part of the Jiangsu province of China, Liu Qiangdong studied sociology at the People's University of China, but spent most of his time learning about computer programming and graduated in 1996.

After a few attempts at businesses, Liu managed to make something stick with Jingdong in Zhongguancun High-tech Industrial Park in Beijing and became a distributor of magneto-optical products. In 2003, Liu had opened 12 stores in the chain.

After the SARS outbreak kept staff and clients at home, he was forced to rethink his business model and went on to launch his first online retail website in 2004, called JD.com, a shortened version of Jingdong. A year later, he closed all the branches and the business became a fully-fledged e-commerce business.

JD.com went on to become the world's third largest internet company by revenue and has backing from giants like Walmart and Google. 

Liu's intentions are to expand the business to Europe and the US after he establishes growth in Thailand, Indonesia and Vietnam.

Since launching the firm in 1998, Liu has accumulated a fortune of around $7.3 billion (£5.7bn) by just transforming an electronic store chain into an online marketplace, now second-only to Alibaba in the e-commerce field in China.
