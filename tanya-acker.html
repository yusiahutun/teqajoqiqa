Professional Life

Judge Tanya Acker was able to serve as a judicial law clerk to the Honorable Dorothy Wright Nelson on the Ninth U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals.

Her duties included advising as well as making recommendations to Ninth Circuit judges about rulings on a broad variety of cases and preparing Judge Nelson for oral arguments on matters before the Court.

She then received a Bristow Fellowship from the Office of the Solicitor General in the U.S. Department of Justice.

Likewise, Tanya was able to get into private practice, and her legal work spanned a broad variety of matters from civil litigation involving public and private entities, to various constitutional cases, to providing business counseling and advice.

She also was able to receive the ACLU’s First Amendment Award for her successful representation of the homeless in a case against the City of Santa Barbara.

She even was able to work in entertainment industry outreach for the Kerry/Edwards presidential campaign in California. Acker worked as Deputy Campaign Manager for the Los Angeles mayoral campaign of City Councilman Bernard C. Parks.

Then, she went on to work as the General Counsel of a company that manufactured emissions control products after that.
