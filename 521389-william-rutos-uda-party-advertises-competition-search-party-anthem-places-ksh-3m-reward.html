William Ruto's UDA Party Advertises Competition for Search of Party Anthem, Places KSh 3m Reward

February 07, 2024
blog
  • The United Democratic Alliance (UDA) Party has announced a competition to create its official party anthem
  • Secretary General Cleophas Malala encouraged choirs and individual composers from across the country to register and participate
  • UDA Party aimed to secure its long-term presence in Kenyan politics, with plans to build a political organisation that will outlast its founders

Nairobi - The United Democratic Alliance (UDA) Party has advertised a party anthem competition.

According to the party's secretary general Cleophas Malala, the winner whose tune will be selected will be awarded KSh 3 million.

Malala said the competition is open for choirs and individual compositions nationwide.

"Register for free to compete and stand a chance to win Ksh. 3Million in the UDA Party search for the official Party anthem. Open for choirs and individual compositions across the Country," Malala said.

The advertisement came against the backdrop of uncountable complaints from the public over the skyrocketing cost of living.

Building UDA party for posterity

In other quarters, the political outfit is determined to dominate the political space for decades and generations to come, and the leadership is leaving nothing to chance.

On several occasions, the president has indicated that he wants to build a party of the future.

A solid political outfit that will outlive its founders and carry the dreams and aspirations of future generations.

True to his words, the president has ensured the ruling party remains on course by planning ahead of time.

He has also ensured UDA remains ambitious and continues to yearn for more.

How Ruto built a formidable party

After the August 2022 polls, the ruling party embarked on an instant mission to strengthen its structures and seek collaboration with other parties, particularly those that had sided with the opposition outfit.

At least 25 MPs from the Jubilee Party defected to UDA, and many others from other parties, such as UDM, joined the government.

The ruling party also courted 11 out of 12 Independent MPs to work with the government.

At least 9 MPs elected on ODM ticket have defied their leader, Raila Odinga and are now working closely with UDA.

This has greatly disadvantaged the opposition team and thrown every plan they had to distract Ruto’s government into disarray.
