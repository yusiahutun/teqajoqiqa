August 13, 2024

Body oils take your self-care ritual to the next level. But they’re more than just an indulgent treat for your senses. Packed with super-hydrators, soothing anti-inflammatories, and powerful repair ingredients, they leave your skin feeling softer and smoother with a more luminous look. “I think of body oils as a complementary boost and really great at serving as an occlusive to seal in moisture,” says board-certified dermatologist Dr. Ranella Hirsch.

Before you go slathering anything on, it’s good to know that body oils should soak into skin, instead of blanketing it, but finding the right one without actually being able to try it can be a challenge. That’s where we come in. We spoke to experts, tested products ourselves, and did lots of research to find the best products. What to Keep in Mind

Texture

Oils have a bad rap for being synonymous with greasy — and some certainly can be. But there are also a ton of quality body oils that feel lightweight and silky on skin, soaking into it, instead of sitting on top of it.

Ingredients

Knowing your skin type will help you find the best oils to use (and to avoid). “Jojoba can be nice to soak up excess oil, as is white grape seed,” Hirsch told InStyle. “Tea tree is generally acne friendly, and moringa is probably my sensitive skin favorite,” she added.

Scent

Since many body oils are made from mixes of different botanicals, they often carry a pleasant, natural scent, which is also one of many reasons why we love using these products. Some have added synthetic fragrances, which is something to look out for if you have sensitive skin. “Take extra care with fragrance for sensitive skin and be advised essential oils are actually themselves a not-infrequent cause of skin issues,” Hirsch told InStyle. 

Your Questions, Answered

How do you use body oil?

For many oils, you can pour them right into your hands and lather all over your body. Just beware that a little goes a long way with body oils (more so than say lotions or creams), so dab a drop on your palms and work it into skin before applying more. Some oils have spray nozzles which you can spray directly onto your body or in your palms and apply from there. Be careful with sprays, as depending on the diffusion of the mist, they can make the surface around you slippery, so it’s best to apply while standing on a towel to avoid creating any accidental slippery surfaces. 

How do you apply body oil after a shower?

The best way to use a body oil is immediately post-shower on damp skin, to lock in all that moisture. You can also apply to dry skin whenever you’re in need of added hydration. Just be sure that if you’re going to layer with other products, like a lotion or cream, the body oil layers last. “They are occlusives, so they mainly function to seal things in, and most things won’t penetrate an oil, so they go on last,” Hirsch told InStyle.
