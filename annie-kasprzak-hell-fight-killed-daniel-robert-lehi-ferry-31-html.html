Murdered girl, 15, 'put up one hell of a fight' before she was 'killed for refusing suspect’s sexual advances'

A 15-year-old girl who was killed and thrown into a river after she allegedly refused a man's sexual advances 'put up one hell of a fight' before her death, police documents have suggested.

Anne Kasprzak's bloody and bruised body was pulled out of the Jordan River, Utah, on March 11, after a passer-by saw blood and a shoe by a footbridge over the river in Draper.

Police arrested Daniel Robert Lehi Ferry, 31, and Veanuia Vehekite, 30, on suspicion of murder; both remain behind bars on unrelated charges yet neither has been charged in connection to the case.

Horrific attack: Anne Kadprzak's bruised and bloody body was pulled out of a Utah river last month. A witness claims a man smashed her head against a wall when she turned down his sexual advances

When Kasprzak was pulled from the river, she was so badly beaten that she was unrecognisable. Investigators had to use scars, clothing and dentistry to identify her, according to search warrants.

One of her fingernails was pushed back and she appeared to have wounds on her hands indicated she had protected herself during a struggle, the warrants show, the Salt Lake Tribune reported.

There are hopes that the killer's DNA is on her hands, the warrants add.

The teen was last seen by her parents on March 10 after she argued with them and went to her bedroom to listen to music. She later left the house and her parents reported her missing.

Warrants now show she spent 30 minutes chatting on her mobile phone to people with unavailable phone numbers before she left her home, the Tribune reported.

Held: A witness told police the teen went to the home of Daniel Ferry (left), who attacked her. Veanuia Vehekite (right) and another man were also present, they added. Ferry and Vehekite are in jail on unrelated charges

She then went to Ferry's home - a place a witness told police she had been before - with a man identified by warrants as 'L.J.' Vehekite was also present, the witness said.

The same witness added that Ferry had made a sexual advance towards the girl, who laughed and said refused. He then 'smashed' her head against the wall, the witness told police.

With the teenager knocked unconscious, the three men carried her to the garage, where they changed her clothes and put her into a vehicle outside the home, wrapped in a tarpaulin.

The men returned to the home a couple of hours later, according to the warrant. 'They all had blood on their clothing,' the witness told police.

Struggle: Police warrants claim the teen, who was apparently fighting with her family before she left home on the night of her death, was unrecognisable when they pulled her from the river due to her injuries

Tough place: Annie's uncle said that she was having a difficult time of late because her parents divorced and both remarried other spouses in the past year

The witness asked what had happened to the girl, and Ferry responded that she had gone swimming and that 'she put up one hell of a fight'. They added that she had lost her shoe.

When police went to Ferry's home with a search warrant, they noted it looked as if the home had been cleaned and carpet had been torn out.

Ferry is serving a prison stint for outstanding traffic violations, while Vehekite has a history of drugs and weapons crimes. No charges have been filed in the case and 'L.J.' has not been arrested.

'We’re obviously investigating the matter,' Blake Nakamura, a Salt Lake County District Attorney chief deputy, told the Salt Lake Tribune. 'We’re still considering the charges.'

Scene: Her bruised and battered body was found on the edge of the Jordan River

Veronica Kasprzak, Annie's mother, previously said the family was 'in shock' and told the Salt Lake Tribune that they did not know of Ferry before his arrest.

She had initially reported Annie missing on Saturday, March 10 after she left the house. The concerned parents later called police after they watched a television report the next day saying a body was found in the Jordan River, and that they feared it may be their daughter.

Annie's uncle Scott said last week how his niece had been going through 'a tough time of late'.

He told Fox 13: 'Within the last few years her parents had been divorced and remarried, and that was difficult for her.'

Annie's stepmother, Jennifer Kasprzak, added that the family thought she had just gone for a walk when she left the house.
