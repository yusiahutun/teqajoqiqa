While the strategies behind achieving—as well as maintaining—lush, shiny, bouncy hair often prioritize recurring trims, your scalp's health can be an equally important factor. "Scalp care is haircare," says board-certified dermatologist Mamina Turegano. "That's where the hair follicle and the hair shaft's building blocks—including stem cells and nutrients—start."

If you're not yet taking this holistic approach to haircare, it's time to upgrade what you're reaching for, both in and out of the shower. Happily, with the recent stateside launch of Japanese scalp-care brand Michiru, that couldn't be easier.

The brand offers a lineup of eight products designed to nurture growing hair and promote healthier, thicker strands, starting at the root. The formulas blend holistic Japanese-sourced ingredients with advanced hair technology and are all sulfate-, silicone-, dye-, and paraben-free.

The Fullness Shampoo and Conditioner—which both earned our 2023 Hair Award in the Thinning category—are specifically designed for fine, flat, or thinning hair and contain senburi extract, silk protein, and volumizing technology. Michiru Scalp Tonic, formulated with nourishing niacinamide, is an ideal post-shower follow-up.

Each of these carefully crafted formulas plays a vital role in cultivating healthier-looking and thicker hair. "Senburi extract is a Japanese medicinal herb that has been shown to improve blood circulation, so when applied to the scalp, it can help promote hair growth," explains Dr. Turegano. "And silk protein has a variety of benefits—it can help with moisture retention within the hair shaft as well as improve elasticity and shine. Niacinamide, meanwhile, is an antioxidant that helps build, nourish, and repair the scalp's skin barrier."

Much as with a face treatment, make sure to work the Fullness Shampoo generously into your scalp to help the healthy hair-promoting powerhouses penetrate. "I personally have fine, thin hair," shares Dr. Turegano. "I make it a point to really massage my scalp with the shampoo—I even have a soft scalp-massager tool I use for that." Next comes the Fullness Conditioner, which is so lightweight that it can be applied from root to tip. Dr. Turegano recommends leaving it on for at least one minute before rinsing. Finish with the leave-on Scalp Tonic, applied while hair is still damp: Just massage a few drops into your scalp with your fingertips before heat styling.

It's estimated that more than 50 percent of women will experience noticeable hair loss at some point in their life, according to the Cleveland Clinic. Whether you're dealing with thinning strands or simply want to support healthy growth, this proven trifecta can help you get to the root of your hair concerns—literally.
