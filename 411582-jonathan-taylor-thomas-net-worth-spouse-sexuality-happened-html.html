Jonathan Taylor Thomas' biography

Jonathan Taylor was born to Claudine (Gonsalves) and Stephen Weiss in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania. He has Pennsylvania Dutch (German) and Portuguese ancestry.

Jonathan's father was an industrial sales manager, while his mother was a personal manager and social worker. The actor's stage name combines his first, middle, and brother's middle name. His elder brother is called Joel Thomas Weiss.

While growing up, Taylor liked the Boyz II Men music group and had various favourite television shows, including Roseanne (1988) and Grace Under Fire (1993). On top of this, he enjoyed watching CNN primarily to update himself on current events.

When he was seven years old, Jonathan's parents moved to Sacramento, California, and he started working as a model for print advertisements. The gigs allowed him to act in various television commercials.

Education

The actor attended Chaminade College Preparatory School in West Hills, California and graduated in 2000. After graduation, Jonathan attended a philosophy and history course at Harvard University from 2000 to 2002.

He spent his third year abroad at the University of St Andrews in Scotland. In 2010, he graduated from the Columbia University School of General Studies.

Rise to stardom

Jonathan Taylor Thomas started taking prominent acting roles in 1989 when he landed the part of Greg Brady's son Kelvin in the television special The Bradys (1990). In 1991, he got one of his career's most prominent roles in Home Improvement, which made him a household name in the United States.

Jonathan later got the voice-over role of Young Simba in Disney's blockbuster animated musical The Lion King and lead roles in other films, including Man of the House, Tom and Huck, and Wild America.
