Boxing legend Butterbean slimmed down from 500lb after 'looking at the end of his life'

June 10, 2024
blog

Boxing legend Butterbean revealed last year how he slimmed down from 500lbs following a battle with weight gain and depression.

Butterbean - real name Eric Esch - said he struggled to cope with life outside of the ring as his weight ballooned to 500lb (over 35 stone).

The 56-year-old was famously offered the chance to fight Mike Tyson three years ago when Iron Mike came out of retirement, but he had to turn it down due to the state of his physical and mental health. Speaking to The Sun, Butterbean admitted that he was looking at the end of his life before slimming down from the colossal weight.

"I went through a stage where I was looking at the end of my life - not that I was going to kill myself, but I was getting down, I was way overweight and just feeling down," Butterbean said.

"I took a challenge to accept a docuseries or a reality show with Diamond Dallas Page and I’ve been here the past four months. And it’s given me more energy and life since I was fighting regularly.

"Now, I’m in the best shape I’ve ever been in my life. I’m at my lowest weight right now than I’ve ever been - I’ve never fought at this weight as a matter of fact."

The fighter participated in a TV documentary with Page, a famous wrestler, helped celebrities to lose weight. While Butterbean could not disclose the amount of weight he lost, it's fair to say it turned his life around.

And while a fight with Tyson fell through, Butterbean has since set his sights on YouTuber loudmouth Jake Paul. The social media star has been making a name for himself in the ring and is currently unbeaten after six professional fights.

However, Butterbean isn't convinced. "I’m not a typical boxer, I go out there to fight, I want to knock somebody out. Jake Paul or Mike Tyson," he said. "Jake Paul runs his mouth too much, he’s never fought nobody, I would love to fight him."

Yet the draw of a potential fight against Tyson remains for the American hero. "But I think people would like to see me and Tyson go at it, which would be a hell of a draw," he added.
