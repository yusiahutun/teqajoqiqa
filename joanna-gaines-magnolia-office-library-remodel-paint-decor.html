Joanna Gaines Magnolia Office Library Tour and Remodel Photos

February 12, 2024
blog
  • Chip and Joanna Gaines are sharing a peek inside their Magnolia offices on their blog.
  • Joanna recently redesigned the library and gathering space.
  • Here's how she transformed the room, and why she chose a "dramatic" paint color.

Design superhero Joanna Gaines has just given us another space to be totally envious over.

The Fixer Upper alum and future network mogul redesigned a formerly boring conference room at her and husband Chip’s Magnolia headquarters. The original area pretty much looked like your standard office, consisting of a long table with chairs and a few shelves as décor.

But it comes as no surprise that Joanna thought the setup “didn’t feel like us here at Magnolia.” So, naturally, she set to work overhauling the room and transformed it into a gorgeous library complete with cozy couches, intimate seating nooks, and walls of built-in bookcases.

Tour Joanna's Magnolia Office Space
Open Gallery

“I wanted this room to feel like a retreat from the day’s busyness, and be a place where the team could come and fill up creatively,” she wrote in a new post on her Magnolia blog. “Since we finished renovations, it’s been encouraging to gather here for meetings because it never actually feels like work—it feels like we’re just hanging out in a cool space!”

Though the furniture and accent pieces tie everything together, the true standout is the unexpected, moody paint color (she suggests her Magnolia Home "Beautiful Acres" for a similar look). But, as per usual, Joanna had her reasons for choosing a darker hue as opposed to her go-to light and bright aesthetic.

Credit: ACE Hardware

“I chose a deep green tone for the room because I wanted the entire space to feel significant and dramatic right when you walked in,” she said. “I went with a gloss finish for all the trim work and ceiling tiles, and then did a satin finish on the walls.”

She continued, “With all the detail of the moulding, the bookshelf and the antique ceiling tiles, I didn’t want a break in the action—I wanted everything to be the same color so that the texture of each material could be highlighted in a subtle yet stunning way."

Okay, we’re ready to fill out our Magnolia job application now!
