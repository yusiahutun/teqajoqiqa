1. Roscoe Village

Roscoe Village earned the nickname "Village Within the City" for its charm and small-town feel. It is located between Lincoln Park to the south, Northcenter to the north, and Lakeview to the east. Today, it is a melting pot of businesses and demographics.

Visitors, neighbors, and locals visit its streets every summer to enjoy community events and outdoor festivals. In addition to the neighborhood's natural beauty, it has vibrant shops, excellent public schools, lively arts, and top-rated eateries and nightlife.

Roscoe Village is among the nicest Chicago neighborhoods with a total annual crime of 601 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

The North Centre neighborhood in the north side of Chicago, Illinois, is vibrant with an eclectic mix of restaurants, retailers, live theatre, live music and service-oriented businesses.

It's also home to some public schools in Chicago, including Coonley & Bell Elementary, North Park Elementary School and Lane Tech High School. In 2014, Chicago Magazine voted North Centre one of Chicago's best neighborhoods. The total annual crime rate in this neighborhood is 1,661 (estimate) per 100,000 residents.

3. Lincoln Square

Lincoln Square in north Chicago is one of the city's 77 community areas. Founded by German immigrants in the 1840s, it is defined by leafy streets and modest homes.

Western, Lincoln and Lawrence Avenue have German bars and restaurants that sell traditional beer and food. There are several Bavarian-style bakeries and shops here as well. The total annual crime rate at Lincoln Square is 1,871 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

4. Andersonville

Andersonville is a laid-back area within West Edgewater. It's famous for its 19th-century row houses, charming cafes, antique shops, and furniture stores along Clark Street, a well-known commercial strip.

Andersonville's Swedish heritage is evident in its quaint bakeries and restaurants. This neighborhood's total annual crime rate is estimated at 1,967 (per 100,000 people).

5. Hyde Park

Hyde Park is more than just a commercial corridor. It's a small, unique neighborhood that embraces and supports a rich blend of business activities, cultural attractions, dining, and shopping.

Overall, you'll love experiencing the neighborhood's storied history of individuality, warmth, and eclecticism. The total annual crime rate at Hyde Park is estimated at 2,591 (per 100,000 people).

6. Rogers Park

Rogers Park is a beautiful and diverse neighborhood on the far north side of Chicago. It is home to an eclectic and vibrant community with a rich history spanning over a century.

7. Streeterville

Streeterville is a neighborhood located north of the Chicago River. It has great restaurants, residentials, professional office centres, cultural venues, and medical facilities.

It underwent much development in the early 21st century, as numerous empty lots, especially in the southern part, were converted into residential and commercial properties.

The Streeterville neighborhood has several public schools. Its health facilities are based around Northwestern University's Feinberg School of Medicine. The total annual crime at Streeterville is 5,441 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

8. Greek Town

Greek Town is a small beautiful neighborhood jam-packed with fun places to visit with your family. It was once home to Chicago's Greek immigrant community. The Greek sense of decoration mixes old and new buildings with incredible urban architecture.

9. Old Town

Old Town is a neighborhood and historic district in Lincoln Park and Near North Side, Chicago, Illinois. It is home to many of Chicago's older Victorian-era buildings, including St. Michael's Church.

Its tree-lined Wells Street features old-school pubs, the revered Second City comedy club, artisanal coffee shops, charming eateries, and quirky boutiques. The total annual crime in Old Town is estimated at 5,914 (per 100,000 people).
