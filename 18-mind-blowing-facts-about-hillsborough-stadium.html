It was one of the first all-seater stadiums in England.

As a pioneer of modern stadium design, Hillsborough Stadium transitioned to an all-seater configuration in 1994, setting a trend that would be followed by other football grounds.

Hillsborough disaster: The 1989 tragedy that shaped stadium safety.

In a tragic turn of events, the Hillsborough Stadium disaster occurred on April 15, 1989, during an FA Cup semifinal match, resulting in the loss of 96 lives and forever changing stadium safety regulations.

FAQs

Q: How old is Hillsborough Stadium?

A: Hillsborough Stadium was opened on 2 September 1899, making it over 120 years old.

Q: What is the seating capacity of Hillsborough Stadium?

A: The stadium has a current seating capacity of around 39,732, making it one of the largest in England outside the top flight.

Q: Has Hillsborough Stadium hosted any major football events?

A: Yes, Hillsborough Stadium has hosted several major football events, including matches during the 1966 World Cup and UEFA Euro 1996.

Q: Is the stadium easily accessible for visitors?

A: Yes, Hillsborough Stadium is well-connected with public transportation options, including tram, bus, and train services.

Q: Can I take a guided tour of Hillsborough Stadium?

A: Yes, guided tours of the stadium are available, offering visitors a chance to explore the history, backrooms, and pitchside areas.

Q: Are there any landmarks or attractions near Hillsborough Stadium?

A: Yes, nearby attractions include Hillsborough Park, the Hillsborough Barracks, and the Sheffield Wednesday Football Club Museum.

Q: Can I buy official merchandise or souvenirs at Hillsborough Stadium?

A: Yes, the stadium has dedicated shops where you can purchase official Sheffield Wednesday merchandise and souvenirs.
