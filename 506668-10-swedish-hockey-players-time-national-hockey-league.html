1. Nicklas Lidstrom

Nicklas is a Swedish former professional hockey player and one of the greatest. During his days as a player, Nicklas played as a defenceman. He is the vice president of hockey operations for the Detroit Red Wings.

Lidstrom was born on April 28, 1970, in Krylbo, Sweden, which means he is 53 years old as of 2023. He started his hockey career in Avesta, Sweden, playing with Skogsbo, SK, before moving with VIK Västerås HK of the Swedish Elitserien. His professional hockey career runs from 1991 – 2012; during this time, he has played in 20 NHL seasons.

Nicklas has won various awards, including Olympic Gold, World Championship Gold, All-Rookie Team, 12-time All-Star, nine-time NHL First Team All-Star, two-time NHL Second Team All-Star, Triple Gold Club member, Conn Smythe Trophy, six Norris Trophies, four Stanley Cups.

The former professional hockey player announced his retirement on May 31, 20212, through a press conference with Red Wings owner Mike Llitch and General Manager Ken Holland present.

2. Peter Forsberg

Peter Forsberg is a popular name in the NHL, considering his numerous impressive achievements during his sports career. The former Swedish ice hockey player and former assistant general manager of Modo Hockey played hockey from 1989 to 2011 when he officially retired.

He was born on July 20, 1973, in Örnsköldsvik, Sweden. He learnt hockey from his father, who coached him for a significant portion of his career. Peter played for Modo Hockey, Quebec Nordiques, Colorado Avalanche, Philadelphia Flyers, and Nashville Predators. He also participated in various tournaments for his country, Sweden.

Aside from professional sports, Peter is a businessman with various investment interests. He was an early investor in Crocs and partially partners with the airline Nextjet. Nicklas has also invested his wealth into Veckefjärdens Golf Club golf courses in his native Örnsköldsvik.

3. Borje Salming

Borje Salming earned the “The King” in the National Hockey League. He is among the first European and Swedish players featured in the NHL. Therefore, he was pivotal in altering people's perception of Europeans lacking the toughness to play in North America.

Borje started his hockey career in 1973 in Toronto as a stalwart for the Maple Leafs. He has played for various teams in the Swedish League. Some of the teams he has played for in his career include Brynäs IF, Toronto Maple Leafs, Detroit Red Wings, and AIK Hockey.

After his professional hockey career ended, the legendary player launched an eponymous sports underwear brand. He dropped out of an alum game slated for March 2022 after experiencing an unusual twitching motion in his muscles. Borje is married to Pia Salming.

4. Pelle Lindbergh

Pelle Lindbergh was another renowned Swedish hockey player who made a good name in the league. He was born on May 24, 1959, and died on November 11, 1985, at age 26, in a single-car accident.

5. Markus Naslund

Among the most famous Swedish hockey players, Markus Naslund stands out. During his career, he played for Modo Hockey, Pittsburgh Penguins, Vancouver Canucks, and New York Rangers.

Markus became popular in the NHL after being drafted by Pittsburgh in 1991. One thing about the player is that he was highly successful while playing for the Vancouver Canucks as he owns almost all of the team’s all-time scoring records.

The professional player’s achievements include being a five-time All-Star, being elected as a First Team All-Star member three times, and much more. Markus is married to Lotta, and they have three children.

6. Mats Naslund

Mats Naslund is a Swedish former hockey player who played as a left winger. What stood out about this phenomenal player was his small size (only five feet and seven inches). Despite this, he made a good name as one of the best forwards in the National Hockey League.

7. Hakan Loob

Hakan Loob was born on July 3, 1960 in Slite, Sweden. He started his professional ice hockey career in 1975, playing for third-division clubs IK Graip and Roma IF Romakloster.

8. Daniel Alfredsson

Daniel Alfredsson is a Canadian-Swedish former professional ice hockey player. The retired sports personality spent eighteen seasons in the NHL, playing majorly for Ottawa Senators. His other teams include Frölunda HC and Detroit Red Wings.
