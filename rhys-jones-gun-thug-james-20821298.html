Rhys Jones gun thug James Yates caged again just 1 year after prison release

April 27, 2024
blog

The thug who armed the killer of Rhys Jones has been caged again - just one year after being released from prison.

James Yates, who is now 31-years-old, was jailed for supplying the gun used by Sean Mercer to shoot the innocent 11-year-old Everton fan in Croxteth back in August 2007.

Mercer shot Rhys outside the Fir Tree pub in Croxteth Park before leaving the scene on his mountain bike.

Yates helped Mercer dispose of his bike, clothes and the gun he gave him minutes after the shooting.

His parents, Francis and Marie, were also convicted of trying to throw murder squad detectives off the scent and hide their son’s involvement, Liverpool Echo reported.

But police hid bugs in the Yates’s home, in Dodman Road, Croxteth and listened in as they openly talked about the murder and subsequent cover-up.

Yates was sentenced in 2009 for possession of a firearm and assisting an offender.

His original jail term of seven years was increased to 12 years in October 2009 after a review but was released on licence in February 2018.

Now, Merseyside Police have confirmed that the reviled character is back behind bars after breaching his licence.

A spokesperson for the force said: "Merseyside Police can confirm that James Yates, 31 years, has been recalled to prison for breach of his licence conditions."

Yates' shameless behaviour has carried on throughout his adult years, in and out of prison, and in one particularly disrespectful act, the thug bid to come back to the same neighbourhood as Rhys' parents.

After Rhys' murder, Yates was banned from visiting the area where he was killed but applied for a temporary overturn anyway.

He had just been released from prison in 2015, meaning this is the second time he has been returned to jail, and wanted to visit a relative in the area.

When Yates was released in 2018, Sean Mercer was the only member of the 11 that were jailed in connection with Rhys' murder, still in prison.

He was 18 years old but had been just 16 at the time of his crime, when he was convicted unanimously by a jury at Liverpool Crown Court by High Court Judge Mr Justice Irwin.

During his 11-week trial, the jury heard how Mercer, of Good Shepherd Close, Croxteth, was a member of the Croxteth Crew gang.

The court was told that on the day of Rhys’s death Mercer had a “determination to kill” when he arrived on his bike at the Fir Tree pub car park having heard rival gang members were there, and even fired again after seeing innocent Rhys fall.
