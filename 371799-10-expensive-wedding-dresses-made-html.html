Colour variations

Most luxurious wedding dresses are white in colour. In some cases, the design could be an off-white shade or completely different in colour. Some of the gowns that have been seen include shades of blue, cream, and pink.

White became a choice that most people go for traditionally because of its symbolism. The tradition of wearing white may have been started by Queen Victoria, who wore a white silk satin and lace gown in her wedding to Prince Albert in 1840.

She broke the norm since white was originally a mourning colour and most royals adorned in red for weddings. Even so, white seemed to be a better choice as the tradition has stuck ever since.

This masterpiece from a Japanese fashion designer Yumi Katsura costs $8.5 million. The dress is crafted from the best most beautiful silk and satin. 3. Vera Wang's peacock dress - $1.5 million

Vera Wang's dress was made out of 2,009 male peacock feathers. Apart from being one of a kind, this wedding dress required the expertise of 8 designers to stitch the feathers appropriately.
