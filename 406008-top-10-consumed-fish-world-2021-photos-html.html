Top 10 most consumed fish in the world in 2021 (with photos)

June 21, 2024
blog

Fish is one of the most consumed seafood in the world. It contains high protein content, multiple vitamins, zinc, iron, less cholesterol, and less saturated fat than meat. Do you know the most consumed fish in the world in 2021?

Eating fish has numerous health benefits. They are packed with omega-3 fatty acids, which help a great deal in battling diseases. Therefore, many households consume fish frequently.

Top 10 most consumed fish in the world in 2021

Which country is the largest consumer of fish? China tops the list of the biggest fish consuming countries. It is recommended that you eat fish once or twice a week, so if you want to get into the game, here are the top 10 most consumed fish in the world in 2021.

1. Tuna

Tuna is the most consumed fish in the world, according to a UN report. There are several types of Tuna in the market today, all with low-calorie count and high vitamin and protein content. For instance, a small amount of 100g skipjack Tuna yields 22g of protein.

However, people are advised to limit Tuna intake since too much could introduce a substantial amount of mercury into your system. For instance, Albacore and canned white Tuna have higher mercury content than skipjack Tuna.

2. Salmon

Salmon is a widely eaten fish in the world. It comes in a few varieties belonging to the family Salmonidae, including the Chinook Salmon, the Pink Salmon, and Atlantic Salmon. A variant of the salmon species called Rawas (Indian Salmon) exists as one of the most consumed fish in India.

Salmon has an interesting life cycle that begins in freshwater, continues in the Pacific and the Atlantic Ocean, then back into freshwater for breeding. Many people love this species for its massive weight (about 62kg of nutritionally dense fish meat). A small portion of Atlantic Salmon, cooked with dry heat, has approximately 25g of protein and 2.8g of saturated fat.

What is the most consumed fish in the US? According to fish consumption statistics by the US seafood consumption charts in 2018, salmon and Tuna were the most eaten fish and the second most popular seafood after shrimp.

3. Cod

From its high sodium content, protein, omega 3 fatty acids, niacin, vitamin B-12, and the popular cod liver oil, Cod remains one of the most common fish to eat today. The species has a unique mild flavour and up to 91 kilograms of dense white meat for its largest variety.

Today, Cod can be considered one of the most consumed fish in the UK, Brazil, Spain, and Portugal.

4. Tilapia

China is the number one Tilapia producer globally, although the Tilapia fish species can trace its roots back to Africa. Tilapia is very popular because it survives harsh environments, crowded space on an inexpensive diet. It is rich in a wide assortment of nutrients, including phosphorus, vitamins, and potassium.

5. Sardines

Everyone loves a can of sardines packed with omega 3 fatty acids, vitamin B12, selenium, and protein. Sardines are consumed a lot in Portugal.

Did you know that sardines can be eaten whole? Although most people hate the tiny bones, they are edible, but just to be safe, ensure that yours have been properly washed before consumption.

You could choose to eat fresh, frozen, or canned sardines. However, always check canned sardines for oil and sodium content.

6. Catfish

Catfish is one of the tastiest fish in the world, but only when cooked by someone who knows how to season it. So far, farm-raised catfish has been among the top consumed seafood for almost two decades.

Like several other fish species, catfish has a healthy nutritional profile. It is rich in proteins, but the nutritional information might be affected by the ingredients used in cooking and the method used.

When buying catfish, watch out for odd flavours or odours. The catfish should have a nutty or buttery aroma. Anything different could indicate an algae-like musty condition of the growing waters.

7. Grass Carp

The Grass Carp is a relatively large fish species with its origin in Siberia and China. It inhabits Eastern Asia and the Northern parts of Vietnam. Currently, China is the biggest producer of the Grass Carp, which grows quickly without high dietary protein consumption.

8. Pangasius

Asia is home to the Pangasius, a large freshwater catfish that is one of the most consumed fish today, especially in Europe. In America, it saw a significant increase in consumption which propelled it to the top 10 list of the most consumed fish in the world.

There have been concerns about the safety of Pangasius species consumption. Media reports have been circulating about the Panga being highly toxic, linked to its ability to survive the polluted Mekong River in the presence of pesticides and other chemicals. Despite this, people continue to consume the Pangasius in North America, Europe, the Middle East, and across the globe.

9. Peruvian Anchoveta

The Peruvian Anchoveta occupies the South-East Pacific Ocean as one of the most caught fish in the world. It plays an essential role in the fish oil and fishmeal industry. It provides proteins similar to those in meat and, at the same time, bears no carbs.

The species has nutrients you would rarely find in ordinary food. It is also a rich source of EPA and DHA fatty acids, which play a great deal in forming the retina, central nervous system, prevention of coronary diseases, and strengthening the immune system.

For over a decade, the Peruvian Anchoveta has increased human consumption uses as salted-matured fillets, fresh fish, canned fish, etc.

10. Kingfish

Properly cooked kingfish is a must-have meal if you have never tried it. On its own, kingfish has a unique flavour that can be enhanced with proper seasoning. The kingfish produces some thick oil fillets, which go great for low-heat smoking.
