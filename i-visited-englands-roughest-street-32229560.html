'I visited England's roughest street and was offered sex within minutes'

August 06, 2024
blog

A former “dark tourist” who visited Chernobyl and was arrested by the Russian authorities has risked everything again – by visiting England's "roughest street".

Video blogger Benjamin Rich explained on his “Bald and Bankrupt” channel how he was “offered sex on England’s worst street" by a woman with a black eye. Benjamin politely handed the obviously distressed Brummie a £10 and declined her “jiggy jiggy” offer.

His visit left him very depressed about the state of the nation. He explained: “You can't help but think when you see things like this that Britain's glory days are so long gone and so far behind us that I can't see how we ever get them back.”

Benjamin first checked into a filthy B&B that which reminded him of the Russian prison where he was locked up after sneaking into the top-secret Baikonur Cosmodrome. Then he wandered along the city’s Soho Road, pointing out the wealth of Indian and Pakistani clothing shops.

A few minutes away, as he walked down streets ankle-deep in litter, Benjamin was accosted by a young woman with a black eye, clearly down on her luck, who offers him “business”.

When he innocently asked the clearly desperate young woman what kind of business she meant, she clarified “jiggy jiggy.”

Social justice campaigner Imran Hameed said that some working girls along Soho Road are so desperate they will accept as little as £5 sex.

"Sex alone would be £20, but they would accept less depending on how bad they want drugs. Lots of people exchange drugs for sex as well," he told Birmingham Live.

"They would take £5 if need be, that would buy a wrap of drugs for a quick hit."

One Soho Road sex worker, who gave her name as Danielle, also claimed that she had been repeatedly raped, attacked and robbed while soliciting on Soho Road.

She said she had been selling sex for money since she was just 16 years old. She is now 34. "I started in brothels and escorting," she explained.

Local café owners say they close early, because the road can be dangerous after dark: “You’ve got druggies walking around like zombies, gangs racing around and prostitutes on the street," one saud. "Things turn a bit nastier of a night-time.”

The area of Birmingham that Benjamin explores is truly grim. Some of the streets in the terminally run-down area have been fitted with imitation front doors and windows, in an attempt to disguise the fact that half of the neighbourhood has been boarded-up.

He said that it was clear that, in the “sixth-largest economy in the world, none of the money being made in the City of London is trickling down to places like this”.

Many of the people he encounters in his travels say they don't know why they stay in the run-down areas that have become their home.

Benjamin visited a number of other UK destinations in his “state of the nation” roundup, and everywhere he found the same sense that Britain's best days were over.

Pointing out one grand old building in Weston-Super-Mare, he despairs of ever seeing a recovery: “Look at this lovely old building, now totally derelict. There are homeless people sleeping in there.

“We’re in the centre of a major British city and homeless people just sleeping rough. You can't help but think, when you see things like this that Britain's glory days are long gone, and so far behind us that I can't see how we ever get them back.”

He adds that in many other countries that he’s visited, the Czech Republic for example, people seem to care a lot more about their heritage.

‘They’d keep it restored, but here in Britain we don't give a s*** about our architectural history. That’s why beautiful old buildings that were once built by craftsmen with their hands looks like absolute s*** nowadays.”
