Bizarre moment former teacher Carlos Hurtado is tackled and arrested by police at a California high

April 13, 2024
blog

Students at a high school football game watched on in amazement as a recently sacked teacher ran onto the track and outsprinted pursuers before being pinned to the ground by police.

Carlos Hurtado, 27, was banned from the campus after he was sacked last week by Heritage High School in Brentwood, California, reportedly for poor attendance.

The history teacher from Oakley appeared to take it badly posting a message to his Facebook page saying he was ‘in desperate need’ of his followers’ support and directing them to a petition calling for his reinstatement.

But no-one was expecting to see the ex-teacher upstage the football as his former school took on local rivals Granada in front of an excited crowd.

‘Shout out to the coach who knocked him down,’ said one observer, ‘I feel safer knowing there are adults like him who take safety first with our kids.’

Carlos Hurtado easily outpaced a pursuing police officer as he sprinted around the track

Police eventually caught up with Hurtado after he was knocked down by a coach

Video posted to YouTube catches the moment Hurtado, wearing a suit and a buttoned jacket jogs onto the running track and leaves a chasing police officer in his wake.

He ran through the stands, hurdled a fence but was finally taken out and knocked to the floor after trying to double back in front of the home bench.

Handcuffed and shoved in a police car he was arrested on suspicion of disrupting a school activity, trespassing, and obstruction, and taken to Martinez Detention Facility where he is being held on $3,000 bail.

Just days earlier he had spoken calmly at a meeting of the school board where he politely suggested teachers deserved a pay increase.

The popular amateur soccer referee who described himself online as a ‘huge role model in my community’ was due to spend the Labor Day weekend in Hawaii after donating $1,000 to Maui Relief Fund.

But his sudden firing sparked shock among the school’s 2,600 students along with a petition demanding his recall.

‘I have been in the Liberty Union High School District for six full years,’ he wrote on Facebook. 

‘However, my seventh year has been quite a nightmare. I am in desperate need of your support in order for me to continue getting back to my community.’

Principal Casey Lewis has refused to comment on the reasons for Hurtado’s sacking, but described his field invasion ‘sad and unfortunate’.

Friends of the school suggested he had been fired for erratic behavior and taking time out to work on his sideline as a travel agent.

The petition was started by ‘concerned students, parents, and community members’ who described him as a ‘passionate and dedicated educator’, and an ‘invaluable asset to our school’.

The petition was suspended after his field invasion on Friday night with one of its moderators writing: ‘Yall idk anymore about this after today….’
