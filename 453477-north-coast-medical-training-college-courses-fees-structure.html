North Coast Medical Training college courses and fees structure

September 13, 2024
blog

North Coast Medical Training College is a learning institution that seeks to offer high-quality education for students that want to join the health profession. The institution has outstanding training programmes that support the best practices to ensure that learners can deliver the best health services to those in need. Although the courses offered are related to the medical fields, NCMTC also equips learners with the knowledge, skills, values, and attitudes that can impact the communities they will serve after their graduation.

North Coast Medical Training College offers higher diploma, diploma, and certificate courses. The courses have a study duration of between one and two years. If you want to enrol for a certificate course, you require a minimum mean grade of D at KCSE.

Diploma courses require a certificate in the course you want to partake in or a minimum mean grade of C- (minus) at KCSE. But since NCMTC has many courses, the requirements differ depending on the course.

1. Biomedical Engineering

Biomedical Engineering is a course that involves learning how to maintain and repair hospital equipment. NCMTC takes on a more practical than theoretical approach in teaching the course.

You require a minimum of C- (minus) to enrol for a diploma or D+ (plus) for a certificate. You must also have done Physics at KCSE to enrol. The fee for the diploma is Ksh 58,400 or Ksh 38,400 for a certificate.

2. Community Health Assistant

NCMTC offers Community Health Assistant as a bridging course for individuals who want to get a certificate in Community Health and Development in the future. Students learn more about behavioural sciences in understanding and addressing public health problems.

9. Medical Catering

NCMTC offers Medical Catering, a course designed for individuals to learn more about food chemistry and how to prepare it with all its nutritional elements. It covers the science behind loss of appetite, nausea and vomiting, and the foods a patient requires to recover.

You require a minimum mean grade of C- (minus) for a diploma (three years) or D (plain) for a certificate (two years). The fee is Ksh 48,400 for a diploma and Ksh 38,400 for a certificate.

10. Orthopaedic and Trauma Medicine

Orthopaedic and Trauma is a course where learners study bones and trauma. Students can learn how to treat severe bone, joint, and muscle injuries and respond to emergencies like cardiac arrest, severe asthma, and shock.

NCMTC offers a diploma (three years) and certificate (two years) in Orthopaedic and Trauma. It costs Ksh 58,400 (diploma) and Ksh 38,400 (certificate). The minimum requirement is C (plain) for a diploma or C- (minus) for a certificate.
